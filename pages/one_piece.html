Manga and Anime

I definitely prefer the manga over the anime. There are a bunch of stuff in the manga that
didn't make the cut for animation.This goes both ways however, as the anime also has stuff
that the manga doesn't, The movies for example. I could go on and on about how the pacing sucks
and Toei is inconsistent with the source but let's not go there.

For One Piece I watch the anime on One Pace, One Pace is a fan made edit of One Piece that fixes the issue with pacing.

My Favourite Arcs

In this section I'll link some of my favourite Arcs in no particular order.
WARNING: There will be spoilers in the descriptions.

Marine-Ford

Marineford is a great arc, This arc along with a few before it set up the introduction for haki; a power system
within the One Piece world. This arc also gives us Luffy's lore, how he grew up, and why he decided to be a pirate.
along side this we also get lore around Gold Roger, The pirate king and Portgas D. Ace. This arc does a great job
introducing a new power system, lore around the characters and the level that the crew needs to be at to survive the
New World (Latter half of the grand-line).

Wano Kuni

Wano Kuni is the longest anime arc. Wano Kuni has exceptionally beautiful visuals and some of the greatest fights.
This arc marks the end of the story for Momonosuke and the Kozuki clan and is also the last arc before the ending saga
of the series. This arc also gives us important lore on Gold Roger and Edward Newgate (White Beard). I in particular love
Wano Kuni Act 3: Attack on Onigashima.

Impel Down

Impel Down is the arc leading up to Marine-Ford. Impel Down shows Luffy taking on a big challange on his own for the first
time. This arc also re-introduces the GOAT Bon Clay. I can't do this arc any justice by explaing it in my own words, So I'll
just leave it at that.

Sabaody Archipelago

This arc does a great job in showing the stakes of the next part of the journey. In this arc Luffy and his crew loose for the
first time and are force to seperate. This arc shows the skill cap to entering the New World and how weak Luffy actually was
compared to his foes. This arc also shpws how disgusting the world of One Piece truly is. It shows the extremely prevalent racism,
slavery, and hier-archy based discrimination all around and how the World Govt. is willing to not only turn a blind eye to it but
also facilitate it.

Arlong Park

Arlong Park is widely considered by a lot of people as the point where "the story gets good". I personally do not agree, i think
the story is great from the start. This arc is the first show the racism of this world in play. I don't have much to say about this arc.

Amazon Lily

Amazon Lily is a very underrated arc. It's the first time that Luffy's without his crew in the story. This arc does a great job in
showcasing the role of every Strawhat member and how important each role is. He can't eat without Sanji, Navigate without Nami,
treat himself without Chopper etc.

Ennies Lobby

Ennies Lobby is an amazing arc. It's hard to put it all in words so I'll just leave it at you have to experience it yourself.

Elbaf

This hasn't been animated yet, but from what I've seen so far it's one of my favorites. Since it hasn't been animated I won't describe it.

Whole Cake Island

This arc is Luffy's first encounter with a Emperor other than White Beard. This arc gives us lore on Sanji, the Vinsmoke family
and sets up future fights. In this arc Luffy as a captain is challanged for the 3rd time. This arc tells a great story and
introduces some truly amazing character building
